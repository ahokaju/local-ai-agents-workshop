# Severe Weather Safety Guide

## Overview

Severe weather can be life-threatening. Preparation and knowing what to do can save lives. This guide covers major severe weather types and safety procedures.

## Thunderstorms

### Thunderstorm Classification

**Ordinary/Single-cell Thunderstorms**
- Duration: 30-60 minutes
- Brief heavy rain, possible small hail
- Generally not severe

**Multi-cell Thunderstorms**
- Cluster of storms
- Can last several hours
- May produce severe weather

**Supercell Thunderstorms**
- Most dangerous type
- Rotating updraft (mesocyclone)
- Can produce large hail, damaging winds, tornadoes

### Lightning Safety

**When Thunder Roars, Go Indoors**

If caught outside:
1. Avoid open areas, isolated trees, water
2. Crouch low on balls of feet if no shelter
3. Stay away from metal objects
4. Get off elevated areas

Lightning facts:
- Lightning can strike 10+ miles from rain
- 30/30 Rule: If thunder follows lightning within 30 seconds, seek shelter
- Wait 30 minutes after last thunder before going outside

**Indoor Safety**
- Stay away from windows
- Don't use corded phones
- Avoid plumbing (don't shower/bathe)
- Unplug sensitive electronics

## Tornadoes

### Tornado Classification (Enhanced Fujita Scale)

| Rating | Wind Speed | Damage |
|--------|------------|--------|
| EF0 | 105-137 km/h | Light damage |
| EF1 | 138-177 km/h | Moderate damage |
| EF2 | 178-217 km/h | Significant damage |
| EF3 | 218-266 km/h | Severe damage |
| EF4 | 267-322 km/h | Devastating damage |
| EF5 | 322+ km/h | Incredible damage |

### Tornado Warning Signs

- Dark, often greenish sky
- Large hail
- Loud roar (like a freight train)
- Visible rotating funnel
- Debris cloud at ground level

### Tornado Safety

**In a Building**
1. Go to lowest floor
2. Get to interior room (bathroom, closet)
3. Stay away from windows
4. Get under sturdy furniture
5. Cover head and neck

**In a Vehicle**
- Don't try to outrun (unless distant and escape route clear)
- If no shelter available, get out and lie flat in low area
- Cover head with hands

**Mobile Homes**
- Leave immediately
- Go to designated shelter or sturdy building
- Mobile homes offer no protection from tornadoes

## Hurricanes/Tropical Cyclones

### Hurricane Categories (Saffir-Simpson Scale)

| Category | Wind Speed | Storm Surge | Damage |
|----------|------------|-------------|--------|
| 1 | 119-153 km/h | 1.2-1.5m | Minimal |
| 2 | 154-177 km/h | 1.8-2.4m | Moderate |
| 3 | 178-208 km/h | 2.7-3.7m | Extensive |
| 4 | 209-251 km/h | 4.0-5.5m | Extreme |
| 5 | 252+ km/h | 5.5m+ | Catastrophic |

### Hurricane Preparation

**Before Hurricane Season**
- Know evacuation routes
- Prepare emergency kit
- Review insurance coverage
- Trim trees near house
- Install storm shutters

**When Hurricane Approaches**
- Monitor forecasts closely
- Fill vehicle with fuel
- Secure outdoor items
- Fill bathtub with water
- Charge devices

**Evacuation**
- Follow official evacuation orders
- Leave early to avoid traffic
- Take emergency supplies
- Secure home before leaving
- Inform family of plans

### During the Hurricane

**If Staying**
- Stay indoors away from windows
- Have emergency supplies ready
- Monitor battery-powered radio
- Stay in safe room during strongest winds
- Beware of eye of storm (calm doesn't mean it's over)

## Floods

### Flood Types

**Flash Floods**
- Occur within 6 hours of rain
- Most dangerous type
- Common in mountainous areas and urban environments

**River Floods**
- Develop over hours/days
- More predictable
- Can last days to weeks

**Coastal Floods**
- Storm surge from tropical systems
- High tides combined with storms

### Flood Safety

**Turn Around, Don't Drown**
- Never drive through floodwaters
- 15cm of water can knock you down
- 60cm of water can sweep away vehicles

**If Caught in Flash Flood**
- Climb to higher ground immediately
- Don't walk through moving water
- Stay off bridges over fast-moving water
- If in vehicle, get out if water rising

**After Flooding**
- Don't return until area declared safe
- Don't drink tap water until cleared
- Avoid downed power lines
- Document damage for insurance

## Winter Storms

### Winter Storm Types

**Blizzard**
- Heavy snow or blowing snow
- Winds 56+ km/h
- Visibility below 400m for 3+ hours

**Ice Storm**
- Freezing rain accumulation
- Extremely hazardous travel
- Power outages common

**Winter Storm**
- Heavy snow (10+ cm)
- Or significant ice
- Or extreme cold

### Winter Safety

**Before Winter Storms**
- Stock emergency supplies
- Winterize vehicle
- Know heating safety
- Have alternate heat source

**During Winter Storms**
- Stay indoors if possible
- If driving necessary, carry emergency kit
- Inform others of travel plans
- Watch for hypothermia/frostbite

**Hypothermia Signs**
- Shivering (early stage)
- Confusion
- Slurred speech
- Drowsiness
- Loss of coordination

**Frostbite Signs**
- Numbness
- White or grayish-yellow skin
- Firm or waxy skin

## Emergency Kit Essentials

### Basic Emergency Supplies
- Water (1 gallon per person per day, 3-day supply)
- Non-perishable food (3-day supply)
- Battery-powered radio
- Flashlight and extra batteries
- First aid kit
- Whistle (to signal for help)
- Dust masks
- Plastic sheeting and duct tape
- Moist towelettes, garbage bags
- Wrench or pliers (to turn off utilities)
- Manual can opener
- Local maps
- Cell phone with chargers

### Additional Supplies
- Prescription medications
- Infant supplies (if applicable)
- Pet food and supplies
- Cash in small bills
- Important documents (copies)
- Change of clothes
- Sleeping bag or blankets
- Personal hygiene items

## Emergency Contacts

Keep these numbers accessible:
- Local emergency: 911 (US) / 112 (EU)
- Local weather service
- Utility companies
- Insurance company
- Family members
- Work/School

## Weather Alert Systems

### Stay Informed
- NOAA Weather Radio
- Weather apps with push alerts
- Local TV/Radio
- Wireless Emergency Alerts (WEA)
- Social media (official sources)

### Alert Types
- **Advisory**: Weather may cause inconvenience, not life-threatening
- **Watch**: Conditions favorable for hazardous weather
- **Warning**: Hazardous weather occurring or imminent
